Unified Championship

Since December of 2013, WWE has had a single, unified, World Championship. Dean Ambrose defeated Seth Rollins on June 19, 2016 (at the Money in the Bank pay-per-view) to become the new WWE World Heavyweight Champion.

2016 Brand Split and Smackdown Live

On July 19, 2016 WWE entered its second Brand Split era. The WWE Champion, Dean Ambrose, was drafted to Smackdown Live, and retained The Championship at the Battleground pay-per-view: defeating Roman Reigns and Seth Rollins in a triple threat match. As a result, the WWE Championship became exclusive property of Smackdown Live. Monday Night Raw, in turn, created its own championship: The WWE Universal Championship.

Dean Ambrose engaged in a feud with Dolph Ziggler, which saw him emerge victorious and retain The Championship. At the No Mercy pay-per-view, in September, Ambrose faced AJ Styles and John Cena in a triple threat match. Styles emerged victorious to become the new WWE Champion, ending Ambrose's reign at 84 days

AJ Styles proceeded to feuded with Dean Ambrose, retaining the Championship. At The 2017 Royal Rumble pay-per-view, Styles faced 15-time champion John Cena. Cena defeated Styles to end his reign at 140 days, tying Ric Flair's record 16 title reigns, in the process.
